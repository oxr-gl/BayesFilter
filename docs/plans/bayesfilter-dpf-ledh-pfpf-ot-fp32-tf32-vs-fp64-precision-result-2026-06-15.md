# BayesFilter DPF LEDH-PFPF-OT FP32/TF32 vs FP64 Precision Result - 2026-06-15

## Question

Can the experimental streaming GPU LEDH-PFPF-OT value path run in `float32`,
with and without NVIDIA TF32 math, while staying close to the `float64`
reference on the same deterministic LGSSM-shaped particle-filter fixture?

Plan: `docs/plans/bayesfilter-dpf-ledh-pfpf-ot-fp32-tf32-vs-fp64-precision-plan-2026-06-15.md`

## Result

The precision comparison hard screen passed.  All three arms compiled with XLA
on GPU, emitted finite outputs, and used matching fixture/config manifests.

| arm | finite | compile+first s | warm median s | peak TF allocator bytes |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| FP64 reference | true | 14.4599 | 7.3685 | 8,606,464 |
| FP32, TF32 disabled | true | 14.9054 | 5.4914 | 4,304,640 |
| FP32, TF32 enabled | true | 13.5637 | 3.1096 | 70,254,592 |

Drift vs FP64:

| arm | log likelihood max abs | mean max abs | variance max abs | ESS max abs |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| FP32, TF32 disabled | 1.6324e-4 | 8.4358e-7 | 7.5527e-7 | 7.1819e-4 |
| FP32, TF32 enabled | 3.5976e-2 | 2.0595e-4 | 9.6289e-5 | 2.9359e-1 |

Interpretation: FP32 without TF32 is numerically very close to FP64 on this
fixture and is a viable GPU precision candidate under this screen.  TF32 also
passed finiteness/device/config checks and was descriptively faster in this
single run, but its drift is materially larger and needs a broader tolerance
and gradient study before being considered for an HMC-facing path.

## Post-Run Red Team

- Strongest alternative explanation: the fixture is well-scaled and may hide
  FP32 failures from ill-conditioned covariances, sharp likelihoods, or harder
  resampling schedules.
- What would overturn the conclusion: FP32 non-finites, large gradient drift,
  HMC energy instability, or unacceptable drift on a broader scaling ladder.
- Weakest evidence: only the value path was tested; the gradient path needed
  for HMC has not yet been compared across precision modes.
